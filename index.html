The Counterproductivity of Ad Tracking

November 26th, 2013

In no way am I opposed to advertising in general, or online advertising in particular. Advertising pays for a lot of great content on the web and elsewhere that wouldn’t otherwise get written or produced. I’ve worked directly on advertising tech products, and I can trace a causal link between at least a few other projects/jobs/paycheques and advertising revenue in general1.

Moreover, if we accept this premise that advertising is inevitable, and perhaps even desirable[^2], I’m prepared to go one further and accept that some targeting can be a good thing – I’d frankly rather be served an ad for something that’s at least potentially relevant and of interest to me.
